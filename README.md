## Project Overview

This project delivers an independent, evidence-based diagnostic assessment of workforce sustainability, equity, and operational retention risks at **Southern Cross Services (SCS) Australia**. Established in 1992 with an active national footprint of over 3,800 employees, SCS publicly anchors its corporate identity in United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)—specifically **SDG 5 (Gender Equality)** and **SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth)**. 

By bypassing high-level, aggregate corporate averages, an interactive executive Tableau dashboard was developed to uncover critical structural vulnerabilities, intersectional pay disparities, and systemic high-performer attrition leaks that threaten the company's human capital foundation.

## 📊 Dashboard Highlights & Visual Architecture

The dashboard architecture is structured top-down, guiding viewers from macro-level organisational health metrics down to localised, systemic root causes.

### 1️⃣ Executive KPI Control Panel
* **Active Headcount:** Tracks the operational scale of the workforce (currently 2,985 active personnel).
* **Total Attrition Velocity:** Highlights an accelerated **23% global attrition rate**, flagging it as off-target against the **16% Australian industry benchmark**.
* **Average SDG Sentiment Score:** Displays a surface-level, self-reported satisfaction baseline of 3.5.
* **Inclusion Benchmarks:** Monitors leadership representation across upper management tiers (Job Levels 3 and 4), exposing gaps with **Female Leadership at 30%** and **ATSI Leadership at 15%**.

### 2️⃣ Geographic Distribution of Employees (Geospatial Map)
Plots the regional male-to-female hiring ratios across the four primary branches (Melbourne: 42.3%, Sydney: 27.3%, Brisbane: 17.2%, Adelaide: 13.2%). Custom hover tooltips embed department-level breakdown charts mapped against a **50% parity reference line** to expose hiring disparities.

### 3️⃣ Total Salary vs. Job Level (Box-and-Whisker Plot)
Cross-tabulates total remuneration against corporate hierarchies, revealing a rigid wage limit for senior female cohorts at Job Levels 3 and 4 relative to their male peers.

### 4️⃣ Total Salary vs. Education Level (Heatmap Table)
Exposes cross-tabulated compensation metrics across identical educational qualifications. Shaded dynamically from light to dark grey based on total employment cost, it demonstrates that higher qualifications offer zero protection against systemic pay discrepancies.

### 5️⃣ Intersectional High-Performer Analysis (Multiple Chart Types)
* **Perf. Rating vs. Termination Rates (Grouped Heatmap Tables):** Cross-references termination volumes with performance scores and couples them side-by-side with an Average SDG Score column.
* **Termination Reasons (Stacked Bar Chart):** Segments historical departures by reason, using explicit colour blocks to isolate high-performing talent (Light Blue for **Rating 4**, Dark Blue for **Rating 5**).
* **Flexible Work Arrangement (Circle Chart):** Plots flexibility utilisation rates across demographic splits, mapping the stark gender divides in policy adoption among top-tier producers.

## 🔍 Central Diagnostic Insights

### 1️⃣ Regional & Departmental Recruitment Inequity
Forensic geospatial mapping reveals a pervasive, nationwide recruitment gap. Every single city branch—including the primary Melbourne Headquarters—sits grossly behind the **47.9% Australian female workforce participation baseline**. Less than 50% of the headcount across every internal department is female, indicating a company-wide hiring bias.

### 2️⃣ Systemic Remuneration Gaps & Glass Ceilings
While junior salary brackets maintain competitive parity, senior female cohorts face maximum salary boundaries capped below the median of their male counterparts. Under identical educational tiers, a severe pay gap persists: **females holding Doctorates receive \$38,328 less than males**, while **ATSI Doctorate holders face a \$14,227 deficit** compared to non-ATSI peers, introducing strict regulatory compliance exposure under Australian pay transparency laws.

### 3️⃣ The High-Performer Attrition Drain
The organisation's accelerated 23% attrition rate represents a severe loss of top talent. Intersectional tracking demonstrates that this turnover is heavily concentrated among the company's most productive assets: **82% of all terminated females and 56% of terminated ATSI staff held top-tier performance ratings of 4 or 5**.

### 4️⃣ The Flexibility Stigma and Policy Penalty
Circle chart metrics reveal that top-performing females heavily rely on flexible work arrangements to maintain high output (utilisation peaking between **77% and 83%** for ratings 4 and 5), while rating 5 males are the lowest utilizers nationwide at **52%**. However, exit logs show the primary driver for these high-performing female exits is *"family or carer obligations,"* proving that while flexible work exists on paper, it lacks the operational, structural support required in practice to prevent career penalties.

### 5️⃣ The Social Sustainability Survey Paradox
While objective financial data and attrition trends point to structural disadvantages, marginalised female and ATSI cohorts paradoxically report inflated internal SDG satisfaction ratings (averaging up to **4.4**). This contradiction signals a severe deficit in **psychological safety**, where fear of non-anonymity or corporate repercussions compels vulnerable personnel to mask workplace challenges and artificially inflate feedback.

## 🛠️ Research Questions & Analytical Workflow

### Core Analytical Objectives
* To what extent do localised branch hiring trends deviate from the 47.9% Australian national gender standard?
* Does a compensation gap persist between demographic sub-cohorts holding identical educational qualifications?
* Are flexible working policies functionally supporting or structurally penalising high-performing, diverse personnel?
* Why do internal employee sentiment indexes negatively correlate with organisational attrition and pay metrics?

### Data Processing & Governance Workflow
1. **Extraction & Sanitisation:** Consolidated internal HR records, active payroll databases, historical exit logs, and corporate employee sentiment datasets.
2. **Level of Detail (LOD) Scaffolding:** Developed custom `{FIXED [City] : ...}` calculation layers in Tableau to prevent denominator distortion across complex intersectional filter sets.
3. **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):** Disaggregated high-level corporate datasets by gender, geography, and cultural identity to isolate hidden operational friction points.
4. **Dashboard Optimisation:** Replaced complex chart marks with clean, executive-ready, colour-blocked data structures to maximise clarity for board-level decision-making.
